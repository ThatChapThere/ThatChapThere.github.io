Contribute Artwork

The ThatChapThere dinosaur project really needs some nice artwork to add to the visual appeal. Anyone can contribute, and they will be credited under the name of their choice and with a link to their site/page. It will be copyrighted under the Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International (CC BY-SA 4.0) licence. Almost any format/medium will be accepted, from paintings, drawings or digital art to photographs of models. To contribute, send an email with your image attatched to ThatChapThere@outlook.com with the name of the dinosaur genus and your preferred name and website/deviantart e.t.c url. Ideally go to the project and choose a dinosaur which currently only has a silluette, or use the reference to find a genus saying "image not drawn". If you have any more questions go to the FAQ page. Images must meet the following criteria:

Be an Original Work

No google images finds or photographs from dinosaur books. The image should be something that you have created.

Be in a 16:9 ratio

This is the standard size for widescreen and works pretty well for most dinosaurs. If your image is not in this ratio it will be stretched by the browser. Examples include: 1280x720, 1920x1080.

Have a pure white background

This is so that the image goes nicely onto the background of the viewer rather than having a visible border. If your image is a photgraph of a drawing or model you will have to use software to create a white background. A soft eraser tool is recommended.

Face rightwards and be in profile

This is so that it comes "outwards" from the evolutionary tree and faces the human in the size comaprison window. Being in profile also helps the size comparison to make more sense.

Be flush to the sides and bottom. If it touches the top and bottom before the edges, it must be centralised

This is so that the size comparison vs a human makes the most sense.

Not contain a name, but can contain a signature

The name is already displayed in the viewer, so displaying it twice looks silly. You are, however, perfectly entitled and encouraged to add your signature to an image.
