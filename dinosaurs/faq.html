FAQs

What is this thing?

This is a rough phylogenetic tree for as a many dinosaurs as possible.

What was the inspiration?

Onezoom.org was the inspiration. The idea is to have a zoomable evolutionary tree for dinosaurs.

How can I get involved?

Click here to find out. You can contribute pictures, dinosaur details and phylogenies for specific taxa.

For images, yes, and copyrighted. For details/phylogeny, you'll be credited on the credits page.

What creatures are involved?

Non-avian dinosaurs. That is, the common ancestor of Passer domesticus, Triceratops horridus and Diplodocus carnegii and all its descendants excluding Avialae (The most inclusive clade including Passer domesticus but not Deinonychus antirrhopus or Troodon formosus).

What's this "Ornithoscelida" thing all about?

While not yet a foregone conclusion, it seems quite likely at this point that Theropods do not belong in Saurischia, and are in fact more closely related to Ornithischians. Relevant Wikipedia article.

Will you ever add other prehistoric reptiles like crocodilians or pterosaurs? What about all dinosaurs (extinct and exant birds)?

Not at the moment. But the layout is pretty malleable, so it's a future possibility. No real plans for this yet though.

How do you decide on how to lay out the tree? Why are no scientific papers referenced? Isn't Wikipedia an unreliable source for classification?

This is not intended to be a scientific work. It's intended to be an informal tree/educational tool to help visualise dinosaur evoulution. However, I have every intention of after a rough tree has been completed filling in important taxa with properly researched and referenced phlogenies. This will be some months from now however.

My question isn't on this page, so what do I do now?

Easy! Just send it to the above email adress. If your question is good enough, then it will be added to this page.
